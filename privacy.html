Quoda Privacy Policy

Quoda is a software application developed for Android devices. Quoda was built by Henry Thompson, the "Developer". Quoda provides tools for users to edit text, and save them to both remote computers and third-party storage services. The "Service" refers collectively to any functionality provided by Quoda, as well as software maintenance and user support services from the Developer.

Quoda is a "Freemium" app. This means that the Service is provided by the Developer at no cost and is intended for use "as is", but some functionality may require a fee (the "Premium Upgrade") to use.

This Privacy Policy sets out the policies regarding the collection, use, and disclosure of Personal Information. Personal Information is any information which may be able to personally identify users of Quoda.

If you choose to use the Service, then you agree to the collection and use of Personal Information in accordance with this Privacy Policy. The Personal Information that the Developer collects is used only for providing and improving the Service. The Developer will not use or share Personal Information with anyone except as described in this Privacy Policy.

Information Collection and Use

While using the Service, the Developer may require you to provide Personal Information, including but not limited to:

  • Google Drive user details, and other Google accounts registered on the Android devices Quoda runs on;
  • Dropbox user details; and
  • Usernames, passwords and cryptographic keys for remote FTP servers.

The Personal Information requested by Quoda is saved on the Android device that Quoda runs on. This Personal Information may be transmitted across the Internet only as is required to provide the Service: for example, an FTP username needs to be transmitted to the remote FTP server so that users of Quoda can log in to FTP servers. The Developer does not collect any Personal Information except as explained in this Privacy Policy. Quoda does use third party services that may collect information used to identify you.

Log Data

If you use the Service and it malfunctions such that Quoda crashes, the Developer may, via a third party service, collect information about the device that Quoda was running on at the time of the malfunction. This data is called Log Data. Log Data may include information such as the device Internet Protocol ("IP") address, device name, operating system version, the configuration of Quoda when utilizing the Service, the time and date of your use of the Service, and other information.

Cookies

Cookies are files with small amounts of data, used commonly to anonymously identify a unique user. Cookies are sent to your browser from a website that you visit and are stored on your device.

The Service does not use Cookies explicitly. However, Quoda may use third party code and libraries that use Cookies to collect information and to improve their services. You have the option to either accept or refuse these Cookies, and should be informed when a Cookie is being used on your device. If you choose to refuse Cookies, you may not be able to use some of the Service.
